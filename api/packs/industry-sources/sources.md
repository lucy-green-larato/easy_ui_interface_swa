## 2. Buyer Priorities (Next 12 Months)

> What UK buyers say are their main business priorities.

- Priority: Increase revenues
  - Approx. % or rank: 57.9%
  - Explanation: Revenue growth remains the leading priority for UK organisations.
- Priority: Improve cybersecurity protection
  - Approx. % or rank: 52.5%
  - Explanation: Protection against cybercrime is a near-equal priority to revenue growth.
- Priority: Improve customer satisfaction
  - Approx. % or rank: 47.9%
  - Explanation: Buyers view customer satisfaction as a major differentiator.
- Priority: Improve business efficiency
  - Approx. % or rank: 43.6%
  - Explanation: Efficiency is a major theme linked to automation and digital investment.

### Interpretation
UK buyers are balancing:
- growth ambitions,
- the pressure to protect against rising cybercrime,
- demand for improved customer outcomes,
- operational cost optimisation through automation or digital tooling.

## 3. Buyer Challenges & Blockers

> Obstacles preventing buyers from achieving their priorities or investing in new technology.

- Blocker: Existing investment in legacy systems/infrastructure
  - Approx. % or rank: 47%
  - Explanation: Legacy drag slows digital transformation.
- Blocker: High capital and investment costs
  - Approx. % or rank: 43.4%
  - Explanation: Expenditure pressures limit new projects.
- Blocker: Cybersecurity risks
  - Approx. % or rank: 41.4%
  - Explanation: Fear of breaches or unmanaged exposure inhibits action.
- Blocker: Uncertain economic environment
  - Approx. % or rank: 41.2%
  - Explanation: Economic uncertainty makes investment decisions cautious.
- Blocker: Shortage of skilled staff
  - Approx. % or rank: 40.7%
  - Explanation: Talent gaps slow down digital projects.
- Blocker: Unclear or unproven business cases / lack of understanding of benefits
  - Approx. % or rank: 36.7%
  - Explanation: Buyers lack clarity on ROI.
- Blocker: Lack of business strategy
  - Approx. % or rank: 31.7%
  - Explanation: Strategic directionlessness stalls progress.

### Interpretation
These blockers form a systemic drag on UK digital progress.  
A vendor that:
- reduces risk,
- lowers cost of change,
- accelerates time-to-value,
- and provides strategic clarity  
will outperform rivals.
